A Hateful Moss

Greenish pallor of a hateful moss,
Emanates from our pain and loss,
Covers a crumbling wall of will power,
A grave of notion, a thorny flower.

Tarnishes beauty, tarnishes life,
Tarnishes values of every strife,
Kills resistance, kills emotion,
kills resilience like a slow poison.

No comebacks if it creeps within,
Only pullbacks from a career brimmin',
Like a chronic disease, can't be cured,
Or a glossy trap, humans getting allured.

Why does it exist? No one knows,
Smothers trust, a fatal blow,
Is there an end to it? Maybe,
Though no one's good enough lately.
