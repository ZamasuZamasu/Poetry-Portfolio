A Lucky Treasure Lore

Once lived a poor man in durn,
Sold flowers to feed and earn,
For himself and his family.

A stable life is what he yearned,
"Alas! It's of no avail!" is what he learned.

Worked hard to pull through his impoverished state
Though his hapless life was a tragic bait.

A vicious circle of hard work for more money
and more work for no money.

One night he was all alone,
Prayed to God with cries of sorrow,

To reward his honesty and help him thrive,
Or take everything with his life.

"Nothing more than happiness I wish for",
"Without it it's worthless to live long"

That night he slept teary eyed,
Hapless to witness another night.

The next day, he woke up as usual,
Going out for his job,

He noticed something unusual,
That old tree beside their hut,

With an intact bark till yesterday,
Had a burrow in it today,

Perplexed, went to look into what lies in it,
No animal, but a paper lies in it.

Unfolded to see a map drawn,
With a cross mounted at a lawn,

Lying on the other side of hills,
The adventurous location gave this man some thrills,

Out of curiosity,
followed the dreaded path of that strange map,

Reached the lawn, abandoned by most people of the land,
Nervous yet obstinate, traversed the entire lawn,

Found a small place, quite seclude,
Yet it allured, the man with excitement,

It had the cross,
The same place which the map pointed.

Yet the man couldn't get the clue,
Until something struck him out of the blue.

Looked down, a weird object he saw,
Stuck in the ground,

With full force, he pulled it out,
It was a box, quite heavy and old,

No limits to curiosity,
Opened it up, jumped with joy,

The box had priceless load of gold coins!
Cried with happiness,

For he longed for these priceless things,
Woohoo, and that changed everything!
